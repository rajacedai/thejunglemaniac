About
me

I was born on August 28, 2005, and lived in a tiny village in Syangja until I was around 5 years old, when my family relocated to Badkhola. It was a really nice environment to learn and grow up in Badkhola. I enrolled at Shree Shisu Niketan Boarding School so I could pursue pursue my education. Even though I had a computer since I was very young, it was worthless without the internet, and since I came from a middle-class household in Nepal in 2010, it was hard to access over the internet. Before 2014, I was unaware of what the internet was. Despite not having internet connection, the PC featured some awesome games like Mario, Prince of Persia and so on, and later I also got GTA Vice City. I remember getting up around three in the morning simply to play GTA Vice City since load shedding prevented you from doing it all the time. About Education, I don't recall much, but I believe I was just above average until third grade, at which point I unexpectedly became the board first in my school. I'm still conscious of that day. In the gallery below, you can also see a picture of that day. Prior to third grade, I didn't give a damn about where I stood in the classroom; I was simply happy to study alongside my classmates. But in fourth grade, things changed because I started evaluating myself.


Before I found the internet and screen-touch cell phones, serious evaluation was underway. My PC was already ruined at this time, and I was just carrying a touchscreen phone. But my God, what I've done with just that stupid phone is beyond what a fifth grader could imagine. I downloaded a separate server for the games that Lucky Patcher can't hack, and I used it to hack every single fucking mobile game imaginable. I hacked every fucking wifi in my vicinity and root-coded my phone. If I had a functional PC at that time, I should have been a good hacker up to this point. I learned about Clash of Clans afterwards. Despite the fact that Lucky Patcher was unable to hack it, I found that playing without hacking was more fun. With Town Hall 9, I advanced to Champions League II while I was in sixth grade. At the time, I was among the top 1% of my school's COC players.
I moved to Pokhara in 2017 and joined Chhorepatan Secondary School in grade eight. Grade eight at Chhorepatan Secondary School was the best. There were some bad moments in school, but compared with all the good moments, it was nothing. Later, at the end of eighth grade and in ninth grade, I ruined fucking everything. I don't know if I will ever be happy again, as I was in eighth grade and before. I can relate to this quote: "I wish there was a way to know you're in the good old days before you've actually left them." After grade eigth there's nothing especial in my life to share with you.. Thank you!!
